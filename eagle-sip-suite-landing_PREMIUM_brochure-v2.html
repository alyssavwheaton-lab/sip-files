The Income Imperative

The Easy Income Trade Is Unwinding

Over $9 trillion is sitting in money market funds and short-term treasuries — assets that delivered 5%+ yields in 2023 and 2024. That window is closing. As the Fed eases and short-term rates compress, advisors face a critical question: where does client income come from next?

The answer cannot be "wait and see." Retirees taking systematic withdrawals, clients with income-dependent spending plans, and portfolios anchored to cash-equivalent yields are already exposed. The yield migration is underway, whether portfolios are positioned for it or not.

Eagle's income team has been solving this problem since December 2008 — not theoretically, but through live strategies with real drawdown history, real dividend records, and a real track record of supporting client withdrawal needs through rising rates, falling rates, and every environment between.

This is the moment to position clients ahead of the migration — not after it happens.

Cash Drag Risk

Short-term rates declining rapidly. Clients in MMFs face meaningful yield headwinds over the next 12–24 months as reinvestment rates compress.

Sequence of Returns Exposure

Retirees drawing from portfolios need income that doesn't require selling assets at depressed prices. Dividend growth provides a buffer that yield alone cannot.

The Dividend Growth Advantage

SIP's holdings raised dividends at a 98% rate last year. Companies with the capacity and commitment to grow dividends tend to outperform through volatility — providing both income and inflation protection.

The Duration Dilemma

Extending duration in bonds to chase yield exposes clients to rate risk. The SIP Suite's equity-anchored income approach avoids this trade-off with built-in capital growth potential.

The Step-Up

SIP+

Strategic Income Portfolio Plus

For clients who want more growth alongside income. SIP+ tilts toward equity, seeking higher capital appreciation while maintaining the income discipline that defines the SIP approach. The right choice for growth-oriented income investors.

  • Higher equity allocation vs. core SIP
  • Greater capital growth potential
  • Maintains dividend quality discipline
  • Positioned for clients with longer time horizons
  • Complementary to core SIP as a satellite allocation
The Diversifier

Global SIP

Global Strategic Income Portfolio

Income opportunities don't stop at the US border. Global SIP extends the dividend-quality discipline across international and emerging markets, providing geographic diversification and exposure to global income trends unavailable in domestic-only strategies.

  • Natural currency diversification overlay
  • Same disciplined income-first framework
